Reporting a maintenance issue typically involves the following steps:

  1. Contact the Landlord or Property Manager: The first step is to notify the person or company responsible for the property. This could be done via phone, email, or through a dedicated maintenance request system if one is provided.

  2. Describe the Issue: Clearly explain the problem, including when it started, how often it occurs, and any other relevant details. If possible, include photos or videos that illustrate the issue.

  3. Follow Up: Keep a record of your communication and follow up if you haven't heard back within a reasonable time frame. It's important to be patient, as landlords and property managers may be dealing with multiple requests.

  4. Document Everything: Keep a log of all communication and any actions taken regarding the maintenance issue. This can be helpful if there are any disputes or if the issue is not resolved satisfactorily.

User Question:
Could we include utilities in the rent?
,
Mentor:

Including utilities in the rent is a possibility, but it depends on the landlord's policies and the specific terms of the lease. Some landlords prefer to keep utilities separate to have more control over the costs, while others might be willing to include them in the rent for simplicity.

Benefits of Including Utilities in the Rent:

  1. Predictable Expenses: You'll know your total monthly cost upfront, making budgeting easier.
  2. Simplified Billing: You'll have one payment to manage instead of multiple bills from different utility companies.

Considerations:

  1. Higher Rent: Including utilities in the rent might result in a higher monthly rent compared to paying utilities separately.
  2. Usage Limits: The landlord might set limits on utility usage to control costs, which could affect your comfort if you exceed those limits.

Rent Hint 1: If you want to ask about the possibility of including utilities, you can say, "Is it possible to include utilities in the rent?" (有可能把水电费包括在租金里吗？)
